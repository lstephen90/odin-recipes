The Best Chickpea Curry

Get ready to have your mind blown!

Have you ever gotten joy from following a recipe? And then putting your own spin on it and the meal turns out to be better than you imagined? That's how you will feel when you follow this recipe. It's a comforting and warming dish with spice and zing.

This dish is at the pinnacle of home cooked meals if you come from a family whose cuisine included curry growing up. With warming spices and a good squeeze of lemon, lime on top of cilantro and you are off on an adventure your tastebuds will never forget. If you feel on unsure along the way, just remember to cook with love.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 can of chickpeas or if you prefer brown/green lentils
  • Half of a yellow/white onion
  • Tomato paste
  • Juice of one lemon or lime
  • 1 can full fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup veggie or chicken broth
  • white corn cob
  • 10-20 cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 tbsp salt or 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  • 1-2 tsp pepper for taste
  • 1/2 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 1-2tbsp of curry powder
  • 1 handful of cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1 head of broccoli chopped
  • 2 yukon potatoes diced or Half a sweet potato if you prefer
  • 2 handfuls of spinach(optional)
  • 1 cup Basmati or Jasmine Rice

Directions:

  1. Put your olive oil in a pot and bring to medium heat.
  2. Follow the directions to make your rice. Once complete, set aside.
  3. Sweat onions for 2-3 minutes. Keep stirring
  4. Add in tomato paste and all of your dry spices. Continue to stir for two minutes.
  5. Pour in the broth. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Pour in chickpeas and coconut milk. Stir and bring back to simmer.
  7. Pour in potatoes and broccoli.
  8. Let all simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  9. Uncover the pot and turn off the burner. Stir in almond butter and or soy sauce
  10. Put in corn and tomatoes.
  11. Now for the final touches. Squeeze all the juice of either of lemon or lime. Toss in the chopped cilantro and spinach.
  12. Serve hot over rice. Delicious!
